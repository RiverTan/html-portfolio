GR 617: Type Forms

Prototypeface

Designing forms are based on the strokes that work together as a set. The characters in typefaces are designed to look like a group—strokes, shapes, angles, serifs and other parts are reused from character to character.
Some forms are based on exisiting character in the Roman alphabet, while others are either be alphabetic or analphabetic. Each of forms can be able to stand alone as an interesting mark as well as working with the others in the set.

Typographic Hierarchy

Typographic syntax is the process of arranging elements into a cohesive whole; the rhythmic and dimensional field in which typographic communication exists. This field consists of positive form and negative form.

Kerning

Tracking is the overall spacing used in a series of letterforms. Kerning is the spacing relationship between two specific letterforms. Many factors determine the accuracy of the kerning tables that come with a digital font: the type of font, its format, release date, the foundry, etc. Some are better “out of the box” than others, but virtually all fonts will need some kerning to look their best.
