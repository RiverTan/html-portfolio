MOVEO ARMBAND

Smart Guide for

Jogging & Bicycling

Intro

As a Means of Validating the Existence of Ourselves. Moveo is a smart armband which collaborates with Moveo app. Moveo aims to help jogger and bicycler to have a better experience in fitness tracking and navigating during exercise. As a Latin word, MOVEO means I move, to emphasize the person who uses armband the object by which the way is guided.

Problem

More and more people start jogging and bicycling, and most of them bring the smartphone to track their fitness and navigate during the exercise.
I found most joggers and bicycler used the armband to hold the smartphone but annoyed by taking the smartphone down and put it back during the exercise. It was disturbing when watching the screen regularly to check where they were and what was the right direction.

Solution

I think it’s a good solution to make a smart armband with fitness tracking and vibration navigation, it will reduce the devices that jogger and bicycler need to bring and make them hands-free when navigating.

THE VISION

A smart guide and fitness tracker on your arm.

How it works

Fitness Tracking

By connecting with smartphone’s GPS, the armband could tell how far and high you go and track all your activities.

By collaborating with the Moveo app, the armband will track a casual walk, an intense ride, and everything in between.

Vibration Navigation

With surrounded motors built-in, the armband could vibrate in 360° direntions to offer a hands-free navigation experience.

By collaborating with the Moveo app, the armband could vibrate faster when you get closer to your desitnation.

Target Market

The target market were adults 18+, tech-friendly, mobile connected, and enthusiatic about jogging and bicycling.

User Flow

Sean opened the Moveo app and checked the Homepage for the weather and temperature information. It seemed to be a cloudy day, and the temperature was appropriate. It was a great day for a ride.

Sean moved to the Map page, viewed the recommended route that Moveo chose for him, but at last, he decided to make route himself. He checked the map, chose the destination, and started the navigation.

When he arrived at the destination, he opened the app and checked the Status page, which showed the distance, average speed, and calories burned for the ride.
Amy opened the Moveo app and checked the Homepage for the weather and temperature information. It seemed to be a cloudy day, and the temperature was appropriate. It was a great day for a jog.

Amy checked her arm circumference on the Status page and moved to the Map page, viewed the recommended route that Moveo chose for her. She chose one jogging route and started jogging.

When jogging finished, she opened the app and checked the Status page, which showed the distance, steps, and calories burned for the jog.
Emma opened the Moveo app and moved to the Map page, she viewd the recommended route that Moveo chose for her and chose one to start walking.

During her walking, she took off the phone from the armband and checked people nearby, there were all Moveo users, she sent greetings to some of them and back to walking.

When walking finished, she opened the app and checked the Status page, which showed the steps for the walk.
