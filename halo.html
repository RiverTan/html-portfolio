Subscription Based Car Use

Intro

Halo is a car subscription service with which user could pay a monthly fee for access to the current vehicle line up. Vehicles can be exchanged at any time for one that better suits their life styles. The subscription service includes car exchanges, warranty, insurance, roadside assistance, maintenance and prescribed mileage allowance.

Problem

The current vehicle ownership has lots of drawbacks, for example, the insurance, maintenance cost, long-term commitment, depreciation after purchase, vehicle tax and registration. The century-old arrangement of consumers buying, insuring and repairing their own vehicles eventually will lose favor.
For the car leasing, it has four-figure down payments, cumbersome lease lengths, and skyrocketing insurance costs: it’s enough to send a would-be car shopper running for an Uber or Lyft.

Solution

We've already moved from an ownership model to the access model in mobile phones, movies and music — and now cars. The car subscription service is bundle maintenance costs, insurance, roadside assistance, vehicle tax and registration into a single monthly payment. And membership is month to month, with no long-term requirement. Subscribers could access to the vehicles via a mobile app.

Q&A Analysis

Why Might I Be Interested in a Car Subscription Service?


• Convenience. You can set up the subscription online and manage it via a smartphone app. The car will be delivered to you.

• Flexibility. You can change cars more frequently than by leasing or buying.

• Potential cost savings. There are no down payments or financing charges.

• No negotiating. The fees are set.

Why Might I Not Be Interested in a Car Subscription Service?


• Ownership is important to you. You want to buy a car, pay it off and be free of a monthly payment for a few years.

• Restrictions on car use. Subscription programs have rules on drivers. And a number use vehicle tracking.

• Switching cars holds no appeal.

• Again, no negotiating. If you're someone who waits for car sales or you're good at driving a hard bargain when leasing or buying, your skills will go unused with subscription plans.

User Flow

Tyler opened the Halo app and moved to the Membership page, he checked differences of the two membership plans, then he clicked the Live Chat, asked some questions about the details.

After the conversation with customer service, he went to the Current Ride page, clicked the swap button, browsed and chose a new car for swap.

Tyler filled the delivery information and submitted it. The new car for Tyler was on the way.
Adam opened the Halo app, after viewing the Start page, he jumped to the Vehicle Lineup and browsed the cars. He chose one vehicle category and clicked to view the details.

After browsing cars, he decided to switch to the Premium membership, he followed the steps in the app, made a payment to finish the switch.

After paying the fees Adam went to the Profile page, checked his membership status and invoices.
Emma opened the halo app and moved to the Map page, she viewd the recommended route that halo chose for her and chose one to start walking.

During her walking, she took off the phone from the armband and checked people nearby, there were all halo users, she sent greetings to some of them and back to walking.

When walking finished, she opened the app and checked the Status page, which showed the steps for the walk.
